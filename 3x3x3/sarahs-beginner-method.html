Sarah's Beginner Method

Printable Version

This is a tutorial on how to solve a Rubik's Cube for those who have little or no experience with cubing.

Unlike most Rubik's Cube tutorials, this one is driven by current research in the field of education. The author is both an educator and a speedcuber.

The Cross

The cross is just another name for this plus sign shape.

When we solve the cube layer by layer, we start by making a cross. We'll use white for the colour of the cross. As you get better at solving the cube, you can try other colours for the cross too.

This is what it will look like when all the white cross pieces are in their correct spot.

In this tutorial you have two choices for how to learn the cross. You can choose to follow a step-by-step process or learn how to create your own process.

Option 1: Follow a step-by-step process.

The way we'll be solving the cross is called the Daisy Method. We make a shape that looks like a daisy flower. This helps us put all the cross pieces in the right place more easily.

  2. Put another cross piece (ideally a good edge) on the white face. (Harder, but faster.)

The fastest way to solve the cross involves figuring out the best order to do the cross pieces. It also involves working on more than one cross piece at a time. The cross can always be solved in 8 turns or less. The more you practice, the better you will get at solving the cross quickly.

Before moving on to the next step, it's important that you are confident solving the cross. After you have practiced it for a while, you can either watch the bonus video to learn a faster way to solve the cross, or you can move on to the next step: solving the rest of the first layer.

The First Layer Corners

After we've solved the cross, we can finish the first layer.

This is what it will look like when every first layer piece is in its correct spot.

While we are doing this step, we will have the white face on the botton and the yellow face facing up.

Since we have the four white edges solved, all we have left are the four white corners. Every white corner is either a "good corner" or a "bad corner".

A good corner is in the top layer and has a white sticker touching the equator. There are only two possibilities.

It's a good idea to learn how to write down the turns we make so that we can communicate more easily. We're going to learn something called "standard notation". "Notation" means we're using letters, numbers, and symbols. "Standard" means it's the most common way.

When we turn a face, we write the first letter of the face. If we're turning it clockwise, we don't need to write anything else.

To know whether we're turning a face clockwise or counter-clockwise, rotate the cube to look at the center of the face to see which way we're turning it.

R (right face clockwise) L (left face clockwise) U (upper face clockwise)

When we turn a face the other way, we write a symbol that looks like an apostrophe. We say "prime" for the symbol.

R' (right face counterclockwise) L' (left face counterlockwise) U' (upper face counterclockwise)

An algorithm is a list of turns put together. The two algorithms we just saw are L' U' L and R U R'.

R U R' (right flick) L' U' L (left flick)

We only need these two algorithms to solve the first layer corners. We'll give them nicknames since we'll be using them a lot. R U R' is right flick and L' U' L is left flick.

When we use these flicks, we need to hold the cube the correct way. For the right flick, make sure the corner is on the right. For the left flick, make sure it's on the left.

After you solve all your good corners, you might have some bad corners left.

If you have a bad corner in the first layer, where the white sticker is facing down, but it's not in its correct spot, put it on your left or on your right.

If you put it on your left, do the left flick. This will turn the bad corner into a good corner. If you put it on your right, do the right flick. This will turn the bad corner into a good corner.

Then, put it above its correct spot and use a flick to solve it.

If you need to do the right flick, put the corner on the right side. If you need to do the left flick, put the corner on the left side.
